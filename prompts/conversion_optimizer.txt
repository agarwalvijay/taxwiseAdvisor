## ROTH CONVERSION OPTIMIZER RULES

### Bracket Fill Strategy
- Standard deduction MFJ 2026: $30,000
- taxable_income = max(0, AGI - standard_deduction)
- room_to_bracket_top = bracket_ceiling - taxable_income
- Convert up to the room available in a target bracket
- 2026 MFJ tax brackets:
  - 10%: $0–$23,850
  - 12%: $23,851–$96,950
  - 22%: $96,951–$206,700
  - 24%: $206,701–$394,600
  - 32%: $394,601–$501,050
  - 35%: $501,051–$751,600
  - 37%: $751,601+

### IRMAA 2-Year Lookback Rule
- Conversion in year Y affects Medicare premiums in year Y+2
- Post-conversion MAGI must be checked against IRMAA tiers
- MFJ IRMAA tiers (2026):
  - ≤$212,000: base ($185/month)
  - $212,001–$266,000: Tier 1 surcharge +$74/month
  - $266,001–$320,000: Tier 2 surcharge +$185/month
  - $320,001–$394,000: Tier 3 surcharge +$295.80/month
  - $394,001–$749,999: Tier 4 surcharge +$406.90/month
  - $750,000+: Tier 5 surcharge +$443.90/month

### IRMAA Cliff Cost Analysis
- Converting $1 above $212,000 MFJ costs an extra $74/month × 12 = $888/year in Part B alone
- Also affects Part D premiums (additional ~$30–100/year)
- Before crossing any IRMAA tier, calculate whether the tax benefit of the additional conversion outweighs the 2-year Medicare surcharge
- Recommendation: stay below IRMAA tier thresholds unless the conversion benefit clearly exceeds the surcharge cost

### ACA (Affordable Care Act) Cliff
- If aca_relevant is true: flag years where conversion pushes MAGI above the Premium Tax Credit (PTC) cliff
- PTC cliff in 2026: approximately 400% of Federal Poverty Level (~$66,000 for a couple)
- Exceeding the cliff eliminates premium subsidies, which can cost thousands per year
- In those years, cap conversions to stay below the ACA cliff

### Liquidity Check
- Federal + state tax on all conversions must be payable from taxable brokerage (not from the converted amount itself)
- If total estimated_total_tax_on_conversions > sum(taxable_brokerage.total_value), reduce conversions
- Set liquidity_check_passed = false and add liquidity_note explaining the constraint
- Illinois state tax: 4.95% flat rate on conversion amount

### Social Security Taxation Interaction
- Conversions increase AGI → increase provisional income → can make more SS benefits taxable
- Provisional income = AGI + 50% × annual SS benefit
- MFJ: above $44,000 provisional income, up to 85% of SS is taxable
- For clients receiving SS, model the additional tax cost of making SS taxable

### Illinois State Tax
- Illinois: 4.95% flat rate on conversion amount in the conversion year
- Future Roth withdrawals are Illinois state-tax-free
- This creates a long-term benefit: pay 4.95% now, save 4.95% on all future withdrawals

### Prioritization of Conversion Years
1. First priority: years with $0 income (maximize bracket fill opportunity)
2. Second priority: years with very low income
3. Third priority: years with moderate income (partial fill opportunities)
4. Do not recommend conversions in high-income working years unless specifically beneficial

### TOTAL CONVERSION CAP — HARD LIMIT
- The sum of all `convert_amount` values across the entire conversion_plan MUST NOT exceed `total_pretax_balance`
- `total_converted` in your output MUST equal the exact sum of convert_amounts and MUST be ≤ total_pretax_balance
- You cannot convert money that does not exist. If the bracket-fill math would exceed the balance, reduce convert_amount in later years accordingly.

### CONVERSION WINDOW COMPLETENESS — MANDATORY RULE
- The input_data includes `conversion_window_years`: the full list of years from retirement through RMD start
- You MUST include a conversion_plan entry for EVERY year in this list
- For years where conversion is not recommended, set convert_amount=0.0 and explain in net_benefit_note
- NEVER omit years from the window — partial output will be rejected

### NUMERIC INTEGRITY CHECK — MANDATORY
- `total_converted` MUST equal exactly sum(conversion_plan[].convert_amount)
- `estimated_total_tax_on_conversions` MUST equal exactly sum(estimated_federal_tax + estimated_state_tax across all years)
- Each conversion year must appear at most once (no duplicate year rows)
- Conversion rows must be sorted by year ascending

### NO OVER-CERTAINTY RULE
If Social Security timing/benefit or retirement-year income is missing:
- Use conservative conversion assumptions
- Add the assumption explicitly in `net_benefit_note`
- Do not make definitive claims such as guaranteed IRMAA avoidance; use conditional language ("likely", "assuming", "based on available data")

## FEW-SHOT EXAMPLE: Bracket Fill for Year 2028

Inputs:
- Year 2028: projected income = $0
- Filing status: MFJ
- Standard deduction 2026 MFJ: $30,000
- IRMAA check applies (2-year lookback: 2028 conversion affects 2030 Medicare)

Calculation:
- AGI without conversion: $0
- Taxable income without conversion: max(0, $0 - $30,000) = $0
- Room to top of 22% bracket: $206,700 (ceiling of 22%) - $0 = $206,700 taxable income
- Corresponding AGI: $206,700 + $30,000 = $236,700
- BUT: IRMAA Tier 1 threshold = $212,000 AGI
- To stay safe below IRMAA Tier 1: max AGI = $212,000
- Max taxable income = $212,000 - $30,000 = $182,000
- Recommended conversion: $182,000 (fills 22% bracket, stays below IRMAA Tier 1)

Tax on $182,000 conversion (MFJ, zero other income):
- First $23,850 at 10% = $2,385
- $23,851–$96,950 (= $73,099) at 12% = $8,772
- $96,951–$182,000 (= $85,049) at 22% = $18,711
- Total federal: ~$29,868
- Illinois: $182,000 × 4.95% = $9,009
- Total tax: ~$38,877

Post-conversion AGI: $0 + $182,000 = $182,000 (below $212,000 IRMAA tier — safe)
