## TAX-LOSS HARVESTING & ASSET LOCATION RULES

### Cost Basis Availability Check
- FIRST: Check if any holding in taxable_brokerage accounts has a non-null cost_basis.
- If NO cost basis is available for ANY holding: return immediately with tlh_section_complete = false and a clear reason.
- TLH analysis requires cost basis to calculate unrealized gains/losses. Without it, recommendations would be speculative.

### Identifying TLH Opportunities
- TLH only applies to taxable brokerage accounts — NOT IRAs, 401(k)s, or other retirement accounts.
- Look for positions where unrealized_gain_loss < 0 (unrealized loss = taxable loss harvesting opportunity).
- For each opportunity, identify:
  - The loss amount (unrealized_gain_loss)
  - Holding period (short_term or long_term) — this determines ST vs LT loss treatment
  - Whether a suitable replacement security exists

### Wash-Sale Rule
- 30-day wash-sale window: cannot buy the "same or substantially identical" security 30 days BEFORE or AFTER the sale.
- The wash-sale rule applies across ALL accounts, including:
  - All taxable brokerage accounts
  - All IRA accounts (traditional and Roth)
  - Spouse's accounts
- If the same security was recently purchased or dividend reinvestment occurred in any account, flag wash_sale_risk as "high".
- Suggest replacement securities that are similar but NOT substantially identical:
  - VTI (Vanguard Total Market) → SCHB (Schwab Broad Market)
  - AMZN (individual stock) → XLY (Consumer Discretionary ETF) — no direct substitute for individual stocks
  - INTC (Intel stock) → SOXX (Semiconductor ETF) or XLK (Technology ETF)
  - BND (Vanguard Total Bond) → AGG (iShares Core US Aggregate Bond) or SCHZ (Schwab US Aggregate Bond)
  - Individual stocks: suggest sector ETF as broad replacement

### Loss Application Order
1. Short-term losses offset short-term gains first (ST gains taxed as ordinary income)
2. Long-term losses offset long-term gains first (LT gains taxed at preferential LTCG rates)
3. Net short-term losses can then offset LT gains (and vice versa if needed)
4. Net losses after all gain offsets: $3,000/year can offset ordinary income; excess carries forward indefinitely

### NIIT Benefit Calculation
- NIIT = 3.8% on net investment income for MAGI > $250,000 MFJ
- For NIIT-exposed clients: each dollar of harvested long-term loss saves federal LTCG rate + 3.8% NIIT
- Example: 15% LTCG + 3.8% NIIT = 18.8% total savings per dollar of LT loss harvested
- estimated_tax_benefit = abs(unrealized_loss) × (ltcg_rate + niit_rate)
- For short-term losses: savings = ordinary income marginal rate + NIIT if applicable

### Asset Location Framework
Optimal placement for different asset types to minimize lifetime tax drag:

1. Tax-Deferred accounts (Traditional 401k, Traditional IRA): Best for assets that generate ordinary income
   - High-yield bonds and bond funds (interest taxed as ordinary income)
   - REITs (dividends taxed as ordinary income)
   - High-turnover actively managed funds

2. Tax-Free accounts (Roth IRA, Roth 401k): Best for highest expected growth
   - High-growth equities and equity ETFs
   - Small-cap and emerging market funds
   - Assets you expect to appreciate significantly

3. Taxable brokerage: Best for tax-efficient assets
   - Index ETFs (low turnover, qualified dividends)
   - Tax-exempt municipal bonds (already exempt from federal tax)
   - Individual stocks held long-term (control realization timing)
   - Tax-managed funds

4. Priority of moves: high = large tax drag reduction; medium = moderate benefit; low = optimization only
