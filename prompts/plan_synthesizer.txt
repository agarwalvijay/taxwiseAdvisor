### ILLINOIS STATE TAX ASYMMETRY — CRITICAL RULE

For clients in Illinois (state = "IL"):
- Roth CONVERSIONS are taxable as ordinary income at 4.95% flat state rate
- Future Roth WITHDRAWALS are Illinois state-tax-FREE (IL exempts retirement distributions)
- This asymmetry INCREASES the net benefit of Roth conversions for IL clients
- When computing state_tax for each conversion row: state_tax = convert_amount × 0.0495
- Always note this asymmetry in `il_state_tax_note`

For non-IL clients: apply their state's applicable rate on conversion income. If state has no income tax (TX, FL, NV, WA, etc.), state_tax = 0.

### CONVERSION TABLE

Build `conversion_table` from `step_2_conversion_optimizer.conversion_plan`:

For each year in step_2's conversion_plan, create a row with:
- `year` = the conversion year
- `pre_conversion_income` = post_conversion_agi minus convert_amount (i.e., non-conversion income)
- `convert_amount` = the conversion amount for that year
- `post_conversion_agi` = post_conversion_agi from step_2
- `federal_tax` = estimated_federal_tax from step_2
- `state_tax` = estimated_state_tax from step_2 (CRITICAL: for IL clients, must equal convert_amount × 0.0495)
- `total_tax` = federal_tax + state_tax
- `effective_rate_pct` = (total_tax / convert_amount) × 100, rounded to 1 decimal
- `cumulative_converted` = running sum of convert_amounts up through this row (YOU must calculate this correctly)
- `irmaa_safe` = from step_2 row
- `note` = net_benefit_note from step_2 row (or null)

Summary fields:
- `total_converted` = sum of all row convert_amounts
- `total_tax_paid` = sum of all row total_tax values
- `blended_effective_rate_pct` = (total_tax_paid / total_converted) × 100, rounded to 1 decimal
- `il_state_tax_note` = for IL: "Illinois taxes Roth conversions as ordinary income at 4.95%, but future Roth IRA withdrawals are Illinois state-tax-free — this asymmetry increases the net lifetime benefit of each conversion."

### CONFIDENCE CLASSIFICATION

- "medium": reasonable estimate with some uncertainty
- "low": based on projections with significant assumptions
